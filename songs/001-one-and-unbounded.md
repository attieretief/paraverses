# One and Unbounded

*Paraverse 1 — Movement 1: The God Who Is*
*After the Athanasian confession of the divine nature.*

## Verse 1
One Lord, one undivided Light,
no parts to fail, no edge, no end;
beyond the reach of mind or sight,
yet nearer than the breath You lend.
We cannot hold You in a name —
and still we come, and still we came.

## Refrain
Unchanging God, the same, the same,
when all our seasons rise and fall;
infinite, You stoop to claim
the smallest good, the lowest call.
We worship what we cannot frame:
the One, unbounded, still the same.

## Verse 2
No wiser counsel taught You good,
no older hand prepared Your throne;
from You, through You, the source of all,
the fount of every good we've known.
Almighty, just, and wholly free —
the ground beneath eternity.

## Bridge
Not endless time, but otherwise —
a deeper now we cannot name;
where past and coming, fall and rise,
are gathered, held, and kept the same.
And we, who measure, fade, and die,
are loved by Love that does not.

---

## Notes
- Threads: *Light* (God as unapproachable radiance) and *the Name* (the Name no name contains).
- Bridge leans into the eternity-as-mode register; can be pulled back to plainer adoration for congregational use.
- Melody intent: stepwise, modest range, refrain as the singable anchor.
